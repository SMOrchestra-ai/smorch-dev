# P.S. Line Conventions

## Why It Matters
P.S. question = single biggest driver of comments on LinkedIn.
Every post needs one. No exceptions.

## Good P.S. Formula
- Easy to answer (reader has opinion or experience)
- Open-ended (not yes/no)
- Connected to post topic
- One sentence max

## Formulas That Work

Personal experience:
"P.S. What's the one AI tool that's actually moved the needle for you?"
"P.S. What's your current reply rate on outbound in MENA?"

Opinion/stance:
"P.S. Cold email in the Gulf is dead. Agree or not?"
"P.S. Context > prompt. Every time. Am I wrong?"

What would you do:
"P.S. If you had 72 hours and Claude — what would you build?"

## By Topic
AI/Claude: "P.S. What's the one Claude workflow that changed how you work?"
GTM: "P.S. What's the one signal that tells you a prospect is ready to buy?"
MicroSaaS: "P.S. What MENA problem would you solve with one weekend and Claude?"

## Never
- "P.S. Let me know your thoughts!" (too vague)
- "P.S. Was this helpful?" (passive)
- "P.S. Follow me for more content." (promotional)
