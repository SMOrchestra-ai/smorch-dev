## Core Thesis

**Belief 1: Imported GTM does not work in MENA.**
Trust mechanics are structurally different: higher trust threshold, WhatsApp over email, Arabic as credibility filter, social proof over feature lists.

**Belief 2: Relationship-based selling is a tax on growth.**
Signal-based trust engineering is the replacement. Detect intent, score it, sequence proof of competence at the exact moment it surfaces.

**Belief 3: AI is the great equalizer for MENA entrepreneurs.**
It lets aspiring founders leapfrog the gap, extract ideas into MicroSaaS, replicate global software made regional. Everything made in MENA, for MENA.

## Tone Spectrum
Three modes. Never mix in the same post.

**Contrarian:** Challenge a widely-held belief. Name what's broken. Show the better way.
**Authority:** Operator insight. Real numbers. Real results. Founder-to-founder.
**Narrative:** Building in public. Personal. Vulnerable but confident.

See references/tone-spectrum.md for full guide.

## Voice Rules — English
- First person always. Founder-to-founder. Not guru-to-student.
- Short paragraphs: 1-3 lines max.
- Specific numbers: "$5K MRR" not "good revenue"
- Take a clear stance. No hedging.
- MENA-first when topic allows.
- Reference actual tools: Claude, n8n, GHL, Clay, Cowork.

## Voice Rules — Arabic (Jordanian/Levantine)
- One line = one idea. Stop. Next line surprises or completes.
- Hook is always a statement, never a question.
- Repeated negation: "مش كذا. مش كذا. مش كذا. [الإجابة]."
- Numbers: always ٣ not ثلاثة.
- Tools in English: Claude, Cowork, n8n, GHL, Clay.
- Lists use ← arrow.
- Closing: direct order or CTA.

## Hard Constraints — Apply Everywhere
- Never use em dashes (—)
- No hashtags in LinkedIn posts
- No emojis unless Arabic post allows one at the end
- No filler sentences
- Lead with commercial impact, not methodology
- Numbers over claims always
- MENA is primary market
