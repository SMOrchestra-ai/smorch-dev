# MENA Vocabulary

## Markets
Primary: UAE (Dubai, Abu Dhabi), Saudi Arabia (Riyadh, Jeddah), Jordan (Amman), Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Egypt
Say: "Arab founders" not "Middle Eastern entrepreneurs"
Say: "Gulf market" or "GCC" for the 6 Gulf countries

## Currency
UAE: AED | Saudi: SAR | Jordan: JOD | Egypt: EGP
Use regional currency when relevant — feels more real to audience.

## Business Culture (use in GTM content)
- Trust threshold higher than Western markets before transaction
- WhatsApp is primary business channel, not email
- Arabic language = credibility signal, not just communication
- Social proof from regional names matters more than global logos

## Regional Pain Points (for hooks)
- "Built for the US market, broken for MENA"
- Arabic UX is an afterthought in most SaaS
- Western GTM playbooks assume email-first culture
- Fragmented market: 22 countries, 22 different procurement processes

## Regional Wins (for authority content)
- Arab founders building for Arab problems
- MicroSaaS priced for MENA purchasing power
- WhatsApp-native sales flows
- Arabic-first products with local payment methods
- Signal-based GTM replacing 47 coffee meetings
